Planet Scale

Earth

If you are reading this, there is an almost 100% chance that you are on the planet Earth. Combine that percentage with the percentage of people currently orbiting Earth and surely the odds are 100% that you are near Earth. (If you are not, please feel free to contact the author to lodge a complaint.) Earth is, after all, quite a large place.

The radius of the Earth from its center to the North and South Poles is approximately 6,357 kilometers. From the center of the Earth to the surface of the planet along the equator, the radius is about 6,378 kilometers. This difference in radii is caused by the rotation of the planet and other factors. Technically speaking, this makes Earth an oblate spheroid, not a sphere. However, because the difference between the radii is so small (~0.3%), it is hardly noticeable for the Earth to be drawn as a sphere instead. The mean radius of Earth has been measured to be 6,371 kilometers.

The Moon

Compared to the Earth, the Moon is rather small. Its mean radius is 1,737 kilometers and its mean circumference is 10,914 kilometers.
